# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We are committed to providing a welcoming and inspiring community for all. We pledge that everyone who participates in the Zatsit Sustainability Websites project and its community will be treated with respect and dignity, regardless of age, body size, caste, disability, ethnicity, level of experience, family status, gender, gender identity and expression, marital status, military or veteran status, national origin, personal appearance, political affiliation, political beliefs, race, religion, sexual identity, sexual orientation, socioeconomic status, tribal status, or any other dimension of diversity.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to creating a positive environment include:

- **Being respectful** - Value each other's ideas, styles and viewpoints
- **Being direct but professional** - We are all professionals, and we conduct ourselves professionally
- **Showing empathy** - Be kind to others. Do not insult or put down other participants
- **Being inclusive** - Seek diverse perspectives and work to involve people traditionally excluded
- **Protecting others** - Alert maintainers about unacceptable behavior
- **Respecting boundaries** - Understand we all have limits to our time and energy
- **Communicating clearly** - Ask for clarification, but do so respectfully
- **Following the rules** - Know and follow these guidelines

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- **Harassment** - Unwelcome advances, threats, or intimidation
- **Discrimination** - Treating others unfairly based on protected characteristics
- **Violence or threats** - Threatening harm to others, even as a joke
- **Offensive language** - Slurs, swearing directed at others, or sexual language
- **Inappropriate content** - Sharing explicit, pornographic, or otherwise inappropriate material
- **Abuse of position** - Using power or status to intimidate or control others
- **Spam** - Repeatedly sending unsolicited messages or advertisements
- **Doxxing** - Publishing others' private information without consent
- **Manipulation** - Intentionally misleading or deceiving community members
- **Exclusion** - Deliberately excluding people from conversations or activities

## Enforcement

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior. They will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

### Report a Problem

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, or have any other concerns, please report it by contacting the project maintainers:

- **Email**: [contact email]
- **GitHub Issues**: [Link to issues page]
- **Discussion**: [Link to discussions]

All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly. All project team members are obligated to respect the privacy and security of the reporter of any incident.

### Consequences

Depending on the severity, consequences may include:

1. **Warning** - A private warning with an explanation of why the behavior is inappropriate
2. **Temporary Ban** - A temporary suspension from the project for a specified period
3. **Permanent Ban** - A permanent removal from the community for severe violations

Project maintainers who do not follow or enforce the Code of Conduct in good faith may face temporary or permanent repercussions as determined by other members of the project's leadership.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- All spaces managed by the Zatsit Sustainability Websites project
- The project's GitHub repository and discussions
- Project-related events and meetings
- Official project communications
- Representing the project in public spaces (social media, conferences, etc.)
